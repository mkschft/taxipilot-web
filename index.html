The real problem

It's not the driving. It's the Finnish.

Most people who fail aren't bad drivers — they misread the question. Here's where the exam actually catches non-native speakers.

  • Misreading the wording — one Finnish word flips the whole meaning.
  • Missing the clue word — kieltäytyä, ei saa, on velvollinen decide the answer, and they're easy to skip.
  • Picking the "good" answer, not the exam's — the test wants the one fully-correct option.
  • Thin vocabulary around passengers, safety, police, cancellations, accessibility and child seats.
  • Losing by 1–3 marks — so close, but it still means re-booking, re-paying and waiting.

Good to know

Can I prepare in English?

Yes. The exam is in Finnish, but you study in English. Every question is shown in Finnish and English side by side, and the Finnish clue words are highlighted and explained.

Can I use it if my Finnish is weak?

Yes — that's exactly who it's built for. Every question is shown in English, and the Finnish words that decide the answer are highlighted and explained, so you learn the exam's pattern without needing fluent Finnish.

How many questions are on the real exam?

50 multiple-choice questions, maximum 45 minutes, taken at an Ajovarma office. Since November 2025 it's a centralised group exam across about 12 locations.

Do all paid plans include full access?

Yes. Every plan includes the same questions, vocabulary and mock exams — plans differ only in how many days of access you get.

Is it free to start?

Yes — take the free 5-question practice test with no sign-up, then open the full app with a free set and practice questions. No card needed.

Is TaxiPilot official?

No. It's an independent study tool for the taxi driver exam — not affiliated with or endorsed by Traficom.
